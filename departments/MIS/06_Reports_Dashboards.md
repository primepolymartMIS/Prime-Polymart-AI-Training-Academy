# 06 - Reports & Dashboards

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide comprehensive training on business reporting and dashboard management used by the MIS Department at Prime Polymart Pvt. Ltd.

This module helps trainees understand different types of business reports, Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), dashboards, reporting standards, data validation methods, report sharing procedures, and best practices followed within the organization.

The objective is to ensure every MIS Executive can prepare, validate, analyze, and present accurate reports that support business decision-making.

---

# Learning Objectives

After completing this module, the trainee should be able to:

- Understand the purpose of business reports.
- Differentiate between reports and dashboards.
- Identify various business KPIs.
- Validate report accuracy.
- Verify dashboard data sources.
- Understand reporting frequency.
- Prepare reports for management.
- Follow company reporting standards.
- Identify reporting issues.
- Understand dashboard maintenance responsibilities.

# Report Validation

Every report must be validated before sharing.

Validation Checklist

- Verify source data.
- Check formulas.
- Verify filters.
- Validate date range.
- Check duplicate records.
- Verify totals.
- Cross-check calculations.
- Confirm latest data is available.
- Review formatting.
- Verify report title.

Never share an unverified report.

---

# Dashboard Validation

Before sharing any dashboard:

- Verify data source.
- Refresh dashboard.
- Check filters.
- Validate KPIs.
- Verify charts.
- Check missing values.
- Review date controls.
- Confirm live connections.
- Test calculations.

---

# Report Sharing Process

Reports should only be shared after validation.

Before sharing:

- Confirm recipient.
- Verify report version.
- Check confidentiality.
- Ensure latest data is included.
- Follow reporting schedule.
- Inform management if any delay is expected.

---

# Report Security

Business reports contain confidential company information.

Guidelines

- Use official company accounts.
- Do not share reports externally without approval.
- Protect sensitive business information.
- Maintain proper access permissions.
- Verify sharing settings before sending reports.

---

# Common Reporting Issues

Common issues include:

- Incorrect formulas
- Missing data
- Duplicate records
- Dashboard not updating
- Wrong date range
- Incorrect filters
- Data source disconnected
- Report formatting issues
- Script execution failure
- Delayed report generation

Whenever an issue is identified:

- Verify the problem.
- Check the related SOP.
- Perform basic troubleshooting.
- Escalate if required.
